Core capabilities

What it actually does.

01
Live order book
Full bid/ask ladder, 10 levels each side, depth bars, spread tracking, and real-time depth chart with animated buy/sell pressure mountains.
02
Monte Carlo simulation
1,000 Geometric Brownian Motion paths per run. Bear P5, median P50, bull P95. Probability of profit, drawdown risk, and full path distribution histogram.
03
Holder intelligence
HHI concentration scoring, whale network map, cross-holdings detection, Gini coefficient, substantial holder alerts at ≥10%, real-time ownership changes.
04
Advanced order types
Limit, market, stop-limit, trailing stop, bracket, OCO, TWAP, VWAP, iceberg, accumulate/distribute, scale trader, beta hedge, basket orders across NER and TSE.
05
Risk analytics
Portfolio VaR 95%/99%, CVaR, Sharpe, Sortino, Calmar, max drawdown, rolling beta, correlation matrix, attribution, stress testing, and Brinson decomposition.
06
Keyboard command line
CMD> command line for power users. TICKER, B/S commands, CMP for comparison, W+/W− for watchlist, F-key navigation — all without touching the mouse.

data company.

Bloomberg Corp. was founded with one objective: give DC market participants the tools, data, and infrastructure that serious financial markets require. Not spreadsheets. Not Discord tabs. A real terminal.

Our mission

Why Bloomberg Corp. exists.

The DC market — NER and TSE — runs on real capital, real trades, and real price discovery. But for a long time, participants operated without proper infrastructure. No institutional-grade charting. No order book depth. No portfolio analytics. No backtest engine.

Bloomberg Corp. was built to close that gap. The Terminal gives individual traders tools equivalent to what institutional desks use in real markets. Enterprise gives portfolio managers the infrastructure to run client money professionally. Atlas gives developers the raw data layer to build on top of.

Bloomberg News and Bloomberg Originals exist because data alone isn't enough — context, analysis, and editorial judgment matter too. The full picture requires both.
