What is the DC market?

For those new to Redmont.

DemocracyCraft is a Minecraft-based economy server where players participate in a real functioning economy — the Commonwealth of Redmont. It has a government, a legal system, businesses, and two active securities exchanges: NER and TSE.

Capital is real within the game world. Redmont Dollars (R$) are earned, traded, and invested. Bloomberg Corp. exists to give DC market participants the institutional-grade tools that real financial markets have — because the DC market is serious enough to deserve them.

The National Exchange of Redmont
Primary DC securities exchange. 12 listed companies. Real order book, real trades, real price discovery. The main venue Bloomberg Terminal covers for trading, analysis, and data.
TSE Exchange
The Secondary Exchange

Redmont Dollar (R$)
The Commonwealth currency
Primary currency of Redmont. All Bloomberg pricing denominated in R$. The Alexandrian Pound (A£) is the secondary currency — 10 R$ = 1 A£. DC economic cycle is 1 month.
DC Law

Redmont has a full legal system including the Commercial Standards Act. Market manipulation and insider trading are indictable offences. Bloomberg Corp. operates in full compliance.

SRI falls 37.5% — what happened and what comes next

Redmont Securities Inc. closed at R$19.08 on Monday, down 37.50% in a single session. Bloomberg News reconstructs the day, analyses what drove the selloff, and examines what holders should watch.

Bloomberg News · March 23, 2026 · By the Bloomberg News Desk

Redmont Securities Inc. (NER: SRI) recorded one of the most severe single-session losses in NER exchange history on Monday, closing at R$19.08 — a decline of 37.50% from the prior session close of approximately R$30.53. The move occurred on volume of 82 shares and pushed the NER Volatility Index (NER-VIX) to 318.99, its highest reading this economic cycle.

What drove the move

Bloomberg Terminal's order book data shows that ask-side liquidity on SRI was thin going into the session, with total ask depth at under 200 shares within 2% of the prior close. A series of market sell orders in the early session depleted that liquidity rapidly, driving price through multiple support levels with little resistance.

The spread widened to over 2.5% at the session lows, with Bloomberg's liquidity score for SRI dropping to near-floor levels. Iceberg detection flagged no significant hidden orders on either side — suggesting this was not a coordinated institutional move, but rather a liquidity vacuum event.

RSI reached 22.4 at close — technically oversold by a significant margin. Historically, NER securities at RSI levels below 25 have reverted upward within two sessions in 68% of cases tracked by Bloomberg.

Holder impact

Bloomberg Terminal's holder intelligence data shows SRI has a Gini coefficient of 0.71, indicating concentrated ownership. The top 10% of holders control approximately 65% of outstanding shares. For holders with significant SRI exposure, the session represents a material mark-to-market loss.

Bloomberg Enterprise flagged SRI-related concentration alerts across multiple monitored portfolios. Any portfolio carrying SRI above 15% of total equity saw portfolio health score deterioration. The attribution engine shows SRI as the dominant negative contributor to NER-correlated portfolio performance on Monday.

What to watch

RSI at 22.4 puts SRI firmly in oversold territory. The Bloomberg backtest engine shows that SRI has historically been a mean-reverting security — sharp drops have been followed by recoveries in most documented cases. However, the current drawdown of 37.5% from recent levels is the largest recorded, and the absence of clear catalysts makes directional prediction difficult.

Key levels to monitor: R$18.50 as the nearest bid-side support in the current order book, and R$22.00 as the first significant ask-side resistance. Bloomberg alerts can be set on either threshold directly from F13 on the terminal.

Our story

Why Bloomberg Corp. exists.

DemocracyCraft runs a real economy. The Commonwealth of Redmont has a functioning government, a legal system, real capital, and two active securities exchanges — NER and TSE. Participants earn, invest, and trade Redmont Dollars. The markets are real within the world they operate in.

But for a long time, those participants had no proper tools. No order book depth. No backtest engine. No portfolio analytics. No holder intelligence. No institutional infrastructure of any kind. Bloomberg Corp. was built to close that gap — to give DC traders what institutional desks in real markets take for granted.

The Terminal launched in 2025 as a Flask application with live NER data integration. Enterprise followed as the platform scaled to serve portfolio managers running capital on behalf of clients. Atlas opened the raw data layer to developers and quants. Bloomberg News and Originals exist because data alone isn't enough — context, analysis, and editorial judgment matter too.

The result is the most comprehensive financial infrastructure ever built for a Minecraft-based economy. Bloomberg Corp. operates six divisions, tracks 17 securities across two exchanges, and serves traders, portfolio managers, and developers across the DC community.

Mar 23, 2026
Bloomberg Corp. ships five new Terminal features — depth chart, correlation matrix, desktop alerts, snapshot, and pre-trade impact preview

Bloomberg Corp. today announced the release of five new features for the Bloomberg Terminal. The update adds a real-time animated bid/ask depth chart to F10 Liquidity, a full 17×17 cross-market correlation matrix, native desktop push notifications for price alerts, a one-click terminal snapshot export function, and a pre-trade order impact preview panel showing projected position size, portfolio weight, and cash remaining before any order is submitted.

Bloomberg Corp. today confirmed that Bloomberg Enterprise, its institutional portfolio management suite, has been deployed to its first client. The platform supports multi-portfolio management, client CMS, mandate tracking, firm dashboard with morning brief, compliance monitoring, and billing. Jan 30, 2026
Bloomberg Atlas API launches — programmatic access to NER and TSE market data now available

Bloomberg Corp. today launched Bloomberg Atlas, a REST API providing programmatic access to all NER and TSE market data. Atlas covers tick data, OHLCV history, full order book snapshots, shareholder data, and trade history. The API is available at R$12,500 per month and is hosted at atlas-production-1438.up.railway.app. Bloomberg Labs built Atlas as the data infrastructure layer underlying the Bloomberg Terminal.

Product Launch
Dec 1, 2025
Bloomberg Terminal launches on NER Exchange — institutional-grade trading infrastructure comes to DC markets

Bloomberg Corp. today announced the launch of the Bloomberg Terminal, an institutional-grade trading interface for the NER and TSE exchanges of the Commonwealth of Redmont. The Terminal provides live market data, full order book depth, technical analysis tools including MACD, RSI, Bollinger Bands, and ATR, a backtesting engine with Monte Carlo simulation, holder intelligence, market heatmap, liquidity analysis, and live portfolio management — across 14 dedicated pages. The Terminal is available at R$2,500 per month. Access is granted via NER passcode authentication.

Nov 15, 2025
Bloomberg Corp. incorporates in the Commonwealth of Redmont — financial data and media company established to serve DC markets

Bloomberg Corp. today announced its incorporation in the Commonwealth of Redmont. The company was founded by beeple02 with the mission of building institutional-grade financial data, analytics, and media infrastructure for participants in DC's NER and TSE securities exchanges. Bloomberg Corp. will operate six divisions: Bloomberg Terminal, Bloomberg Enterprise, Bloomberg Atlas, Bloomberg Labs, Bloomberg News, and Bloomberg Originals.
