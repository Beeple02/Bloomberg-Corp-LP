About Bloomberg Corp.

Bloomberg Corp. builds the financial infrastructure DC markets run on.

Founded in 2026. Six divisions. Registered in the Commonwealth of Redmont.

We exist because DC's markets deserve institutional-grade tools.

DemocracyCraft runs a real economy. The NER and TSE exchanges have real capital, real price discovery, and real participants — but for a long time, those participants operated without proper infrastructure. Bloomberg Corp. was founded to close that gap.

Our story

The Bloomberg Terminal launched in March 2026 as a Flask application with live NER data integration — 14 pages of institutional-grade market analysis, order execution, and portfolio management tools. It was the first serious trading infrastructure ever built for a Minecraft-based economy.

Bloomberg Enterprise followed as the platform scaled to serve portfolio managers running capital on behalf of clients. Bloomberg Atlas opened the raw data layer to developers and quants building on top of Bloomberg data. Bloomberg News and Bloomberg Originals exist because data alone isn't enough — context, analysis, and editorial judgment matter too.

Bloomberg Labs — the internal engineering division — builds and maintains all of it. Three thousand five hundred lines of Flask. A full quantitative engine. Real-time streaming via server-sent events. All deployed on Railway infrastructure with persistent storage for enterprise state.

SRI falls 37.5% — what happened and what comes next

Redmont Securities Inc. closed at R$19.08 on Monday, down 37.50% in a single session. Bloomberg News reconstructs the day, analyses what drove the selloff, and examines what holders should watch.

Bloomberg News · March 23, 2026 · Bloomberg News Desk

Redmont Securities Inc. (NER: SRI) recorded one of the most severe single-session losses in NER exchange history on Monday, closing at R$19.08 — a decline of 37.50% from the prior session close of approximately R$30.53. The move occurred on volume of 82 shares and pushed the NER Volatility Index (NER-VIX) to 318.99, its highest reading this economic cycle.

What drove the move

Bloomberg Terminal's order book data shows that ask-side liquidity on SRI was thin going into the session, with total ask depth at under 200 shares within 2% of the prior close. A series of market sell orders in the early session depleted that liquidity rapidly, driving price through multiple support levels with little resistance.

The spread widened to over 2.5% at the session lows, with Bloomberg's liquidity score for SRI dropping to near-floor levels. Iceberg detection flagged no significant hidden orders on either side — suggesting this was not a coordinated institutional move, but rather a liquidity vacuum event.

RSI reached 22.4 at close — technically oversold by a significant margin. Historically, NER securities at RSI levels below 25 have reverted upward within two sessions in 68% of documented cases.

Holder impact

Bloomberg Terminal's holder intelligence data shows SRI has a Gini coefficient of 0.71, indicating concentrated ownership. The top 10% of holders control approximately 65% of outstanding shares. Bloomberg Enterprise flagged SRI-related concentration alerts across multiple monitored portfolios on Monday.

What to watch

RSI at 22.4 puts SRI firmly in oversold territory. Key levels: R$18.50 as the nearest bid-side support in the current order book, and R$22.00 as the first significant ask-side resistance. Bloomberg alerts can be set on either threshold directly from F13 on the terminal.
